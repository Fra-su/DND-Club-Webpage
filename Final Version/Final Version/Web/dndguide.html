Basic Concepts

In D&D, everyone uses dices. The most common one is the D20, which is a 20 sided dice. One person will be the DM, meaning Dungeon Master. The DM is like the narrator; he will tell you what happens in the game as your characters interact with the world. Every time you make an action, the DM will ask you to roll a D20 for a specific stat. If you roll a high number, you are relatively successful, and if you roll a low number, you're not. Then you add your Stat modifier to your roll. (more on that later)
For example, if the DM asks Bruce to roll for persuasion to persuade someone to do something and he rolls a 17, he adds 2 (His stat modifier) and gets 19. 19 is a relatively high number in the range of 1-20, so he succeeds in persuading that guy. There are many types of dices such as D4, D6, D8, D10, D12, and of course D20 and they all have their uses. You have 6 stat areas: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Wisdom, Intelligence, and Charisma. There are specific stats under those areas such as Acrobatics is under Dexterity. If you have a high Dexterity value (which means you are proficient in dexterity) those stats under it would also have a higher modifier, giving you a higher chance to succeed at your roll.


Classes and Actions

In D&D, you play as a fantasy character. Before you even begin a campaign of D&D, you have to create that character first. There are websites such as D&D Beyond to help you with that, but you can create it on a Character Sheet as well. You first will have to choose a class.
Classes dictates your playstyle and stats. Different classes have different stat proficiencies, such as Rogue, a sneaky class that darts around, has proficiency in Dexterity. Spellcasting classes such as Wizard and Druid will have cantrips and spells that they could use. Spells use 'Spellslots'. Every time you use a spell, it consumes one spell slot. You can regain those spell slots by having a Long Rest, which is defined as doing basically nothing but resting for 6 whole hours. Cantrips are like spells, but they don't consume spell slots and are less powerful. In combat, use actions to attack. Everyone within the battle will take turns taking Actions.


Summary

D&D is a unique TTRPG (Tabletop Role Playing Game) freedom is guarenteed. Do you want to light the barman at a tavern on fire? Sure! Roll for damage. Want to try tame this dragon that was supposed to be the final boss? Sure! (This one might be a little harder though)
D&D allows you to do anything. There are infinite possibilities while playing D&D. It's the reason why so many people enjoy playing it, and you might too!
